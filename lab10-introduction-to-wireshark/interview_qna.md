Q1. What is Wireshark used for in cybersecurity?  
A. Capturing and analyzing network packets for monitoring and forensics.

Q2. Why was ens5 selected?  
A. It is the primary active network interface.

Q3. What is tshark?  
A. Command-line version of Wireshark.

Q4. Why set special permissions on dumpcap?  
A. To capture packets without running as root.

Q5. What does an HTTP filter do?  
A. Isolates HTTP traffic.

Q6. How are DNS issues identified?  
A. Using DNS query and response filters.

Q7. What does tcp.flags.syn == 1 indicate?  
A. TCP connection initiation attempts.

Q8. Why is filtering important?  
A. Reduces noise and improves analysis efficiency.

Q9. Why export filtered traffic?  
A. For focused analysis and reporting.

Q10. Why is packet-level visibility important?  
A. It helps reconstruct attacks and identify malicious activity.
