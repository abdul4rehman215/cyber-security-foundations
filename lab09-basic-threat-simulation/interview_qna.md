Q1. What is the goal of a basic threat simulation?  
A. To identify open ports and services and assess security risks.

Q2. Why was 127.0.0.1 used?  
A. It is a safe, authorized local testing target.

Q3. What does nmap -sV do?  
A. Detects service versions on open ports.

Q4. Why start SSH and Apache services?  
A. To simulate real-world services for enumeration.

Q5. What does nmap -A perform?  
A. Aggressive scanning including OS and scripts.

Q6. Why use --script vuln?  
A. To automate vulnerability checks.

Q7. How did Python help?  
A. Automated scanning, logging, and reporting.

Q8. Why is logging important?  
A. For auditing and investigation.

Q9. Why save results in JSON and reports?  
A. For structured security documentation.

Q10. Why is authorization critical?  
A. Unauthorized scanning is illegal and unethical.
