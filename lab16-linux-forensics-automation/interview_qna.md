Q1. What is Linux forensics?
A. The collection, preservation, analysis, and reporting of evidence from Linux systems.

Q2. Why automate forensic collection?
A. To ensure repeatability, speed, and reduced human error.

Q3. What artefacts were collected?
A. Logs, processes, network data, users, and system information.

Q4. Why use structured directories?
A. To preserve integrity and simplify analysis.

Q5. How did automation help SOC workflows?
A. Faster evidence capture during incidents.

Q6. What suspicious behavior was detected?
A. Brute-force attempts and abnormal listening ports.

Q7. Why is log analysis important?
A. It reveals attacker behavior and timelines.

Q8. What role does network analysis play?
A. Identifies backdoors and C2 activity.

Q9. Why are forensic reports critical?
A. They support audits, response, and legal needs.

Q10. How does this lab help DFIR careers?
A. It demonstrates real-world forensic automation and analysis.
