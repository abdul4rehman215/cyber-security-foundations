Q1. What is the purpose of UFW?
A. To control incoming and outgoing network traffic.

Q2. Why allow SSH before enabling UFW?
A. To prevent accidental lockout.

Q3. What does default deny incoming do?
A. Blocks all inbound connections by default.

Q4. Why change SSH port?
A. Reduces automated brute-force attempts.

Q5. Why disable root login?
A. Prevents direct root compromise.

Q6. Why use SSH keys?
A. Stronger authentication than passwords.

Q7. What is journalctl used for?
A. Monitoring system and security logs.

Q8. Why monitor failed SSH logins?
A. Detect brute-force or intrusion attempts.

Q9. Purpose of automation scripts?
A. Continuous, hands-off security monitoring.

Q10. How does cron help security?
A. Schedules regular security checks automatically.
